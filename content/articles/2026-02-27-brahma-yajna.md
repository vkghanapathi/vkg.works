abstract: A detailed analytical commentary on the Śvādhyāya Brāhmaṇam (Study of the
  Vedas) and its associated mantras, comprising seven anuvakas (sections) that systematize
  the ritual prescriptions, bodily postures, purificatory procedures, and mantra recitations
  required for daily Vedic self-study (svādhyāya). This work codifies both the vidhi
  (procedural rules) and arthavāda (explanatory rationales) governing the Brahmayajña
  (daily sacrifice of study), serving as a comprehensive liturgical and exegetical
  manual for practitioners of Vedic dharma.
preamble: This text belongs to the Brāhmana literature tradition attached to the Vedas,
  specifically addressing the daily domestic ritual of svādhyāya (self-study and recitation
  of sacred texts). The Brahmayajña constitutes one of the five daily obligatory sacrifices
  (pañcamahāyajña) prescribed in Hindu dharmaśāstra for all twice-born householders.
  The seven sections progress systematically through temporal (dawn recitation, midday
  intensification), spatial (cardinal orientation), somatic (ritual purification and
  āsana positioning), and mantric dimensions, integrating citations from the Upaniṣads
  (particularly the Mahānārāyaṇa and Jābāla passages) to establish philosophical foundations
  for the practice.
