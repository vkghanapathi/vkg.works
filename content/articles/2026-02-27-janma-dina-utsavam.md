abstract: This technical article examines the sacred significance of the birth anniversary
  (Janma Dina Utsavam) of a revered spiritual teacher (Sadguru) within the framework
  of Vedic timekeeping and life-review practices. Ghanapathi argues that birth anniversaries,
  grounded in classical Hindu astronomy and ethics, serve as occasions for structured
  introspection, spiritual rededication, and rational life-planning rather than secular
  celebration, with particular emphasis on how enlightened souls exemplify this tradition.
preamble: This work is situated within the Hindu philosophical and ritualistic traditions
  of Dharma Śāstra (ethical codes) and Jyotiṣa (Vedic astrology). It draws on classical
  systems of temporal measurement derived from celestial observation and the doctrine
  of Karma and Janma (actions and births across lifetimes). The Jaātaka Chakram (natal
  horoscope) and life-cycle rituals form the scriptural foundation for understanding
  birth anniversaries not as secular festivals but as observances prescribed for moral
  self-assessment and spiritual progress, practices that have been central to Hindu
  life-conduct for millennia.
