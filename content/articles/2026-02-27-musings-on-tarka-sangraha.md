abstract: This analytical article examines the neuroscientific mechanisms underlying
  human perception of complex visual and auditory scenes. Through hierarchical processing
  models, the work traces sensory capture, neural transmission, and cortical integration
  across specialized brain regions, demonstrating how the brain rapidly constructs
  meaningful environmental representations from raw sensory input.
preamble: This work belongs to the contemporary Western scientific tradition of cognitive
  neuroscience and perceptual psychology. Rather than emerging from Vedic, Sanskrit,
  or classical Indian textual traditions, it represents modern empirical investigation
  into sensory processing using methods from experimental neuroscience. The article
  draws on principles established through functional neuroimaging (fMRI), electrophysiology,
  and behavioral studies conducted primarily in English-language academic contexts
  since the late 20th century.
