abstract: This technical article examines the Vedic and dharmic foundations of birth
  anniversary celebrations, particularly in the context of honoring spiritual teachers
  (Sadguru). Dr. Ghanapāṭhī traces the development of temporal measurement systems
  in ancient Indian astronomy and their integration with astrological practice (Jaataka
  Chakram/Kundali), demonstrating how the birth anniversary evolved as a structured
  occasion for self-assessment, spiritual recommitment, and material stewardship rather
  than secular festivity.
preamble: Rooted in the Vedic-Puranic tradition and the Dharma Shastras, this work
  draws on classical Indian systems of time-reckoning (Brahmanda and Pindanda calculations)
  and astrological forecasting to reclaim the philosophical significance of birth
  anniversaries within Hindu spiritual practice. The article addresses the contemporary
  secularization of birthday celebrations by restoring their original dharmic purpose—annual
  performance review, introspection (Atma-vicara), and life-realignment toward spiritual
  and material progress—particularly relevant in venerating enlightened teachers (Sadguru)
  whose births are commemorated as sacred occasions.
