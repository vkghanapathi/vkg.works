abstract: A scholarly exegesis of Yajurveda (Taittirīya Saṃhitā 2.6) and related Brāhmaṇa
  passages, this speech isolates foundational principles governing Vedic ritual purification
  and sanctification. Dr. Ghanapāṭhī systematically presents doctrinal and procedural
  rules—drawn from the Taittirīya Brāhmaṇa and associated commentarial traditions—governing
  the ritual use of water, ghee, oblations, and the ethical consequences of ritual
  impropriety, establishing the philosophical and practical hierarchy of legitimate
  recipients and offerings.
preamble: 'This work belongs to the Yajurveda exegetical tradition, specifically the
  Taittirīya recension (Taittirīya Saṃhitā and Taittirīya Brāhmaṇa), foundational
  texts of Vedic ritual knowledge and Brahmanical theology. The speech addresses core
  ritual concepts: the sanctifying role of water (ap) as primordial substance, the
  association of ghee with cosmic order and divine truth (satya), and the ethical
  doctrine that improper ritual conduct generates karmic debt binding the performer
  to lower realms. These passages instantiate Vedic philosophy''s integration of cosmological
  principle, ritual action, and moral consequence.'
