abstract: This analytical essay applies the principles of Sandhya Upaasana (daily
  ritual observance and meditation practice) to address the contemporary malaise of
  chronic overwork and mental exhaustion in modern society. Dr. Ghanapathi argues
  that traditional Vedic practices of cessation and self-maintenance offer a corrective
  framework grounded in Ayurvedic principles of bodily and mental conservation, proposing
  that deliberate rest and ritual pause are essential to a sustainable life course.
preamble: Sandhya Upaasana represents a foundational daily practice in Vedic Hinduism,
  traditionally performed at dawn and dusk as a spiritual observance combining meditation,
  breath regulation, and mental discipline. The practice embodies the principle of
  ritualized cessation and mindful pause within the rhythms of daily life. This work
  recontextualizes Sandhya Upaasana not as a merely religious duty but as a psychological
  and physiological necessity aligned with Ayurvedic health science, demonstrating
  the continued relevance of Vedic practice to modern wellness concerns.
