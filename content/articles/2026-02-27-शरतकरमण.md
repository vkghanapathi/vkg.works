abstract: This analytical study examines twenty-one ritual complexes (yajña-saṃsthānāḥ)
  systematized within the Śrautakarmasu framework, drawing on the classification scheme
  of Gautama Dharma Sutra and the procedural guidelines of Apastamba Kalpa Sutra.
  The work establishes the doctrinal foundations of Vedic ritual practice by organizing
  the principal sacrifice types—seven pāka-yajñas, seven havis-yajñas, and seven soma-yajñas—and
  their theological significance within the maintenance of cosmic order and individual
  purification.
preamble: This work situates itself within the Vedic ritual tradition (Śrautakarmasu)
  that forms the doctrinal core of Sanātana Dharma. Drawing from the Kṛṣṇa-Yajurveda
  and its Taittirīya recension, it relies on the Apastamba Kalpa Sutra—a foundational
  text that codifies ritual procedures for Vedic practitioners. The study addresses
  the necessity of Kalpa Sūtras as interpretive frameworks (likened to the hands of
  the body) without which the dispersed ritual injunctions scattered throughout the
  Vedas and Brāhmaṇas cannot be coherently understood or enacted. This analytical
  approach bridges scriptural authority and practical performance, essential for maintaining
  the Vedic tradition in its complete form.
