Brahmāṇḍa Purāṇa – Dattātreya Janma Varṇanam

ब्रह्माण्डपुराणम् – उत्तरखण्डः

दत्तात्रेयचरितम् (अध्यायाः ३८–४२)
अध्यायः ३८ – दत्तात्रेयजन्मवर्णनम्


Verse Devanāgarī IAST English Translation
1 शौनक उवाच — कथं दत्तात्रेयो जातः कस्य पुत्रः स उच्यते । किं तपः कृतवान् विप्रः केन वा वरलिप्सया ॥ śaunaka uvāca — kathaṃ dattātreyo jātaḥ kasya putraḥ sa ucyate | kiṃ tapaḥ kṛtavān vipraḥ kena vā varalipsayā || Shaunaka said: How was Dattātreya born? Whose son is he said to be? What penance did the sage perform, and for what boon did he aspire?
2 सूत उवाच — शृणु शौनक तत्त्वेन यथा दत्तात्रेयोऽभवत् । अत्रेरनसूयायां वै त्रिमूर्तिस्वरूपधृक् ॥ sūta uvāca — śṛṇu śaunaka tattvena yathā dattātreyo'bhavat | atreranasyāyāṃ vai trimūrtisvarūpadhṛk || Sūta said: Listen, O Shaunaka, to the truth of how Dattātreya came to be — the bearer of the Trimurti form, born to Atri and Anasuya.
3 अत्रिः प्रजापतिः श्रीमान् अनसूया पतिव्रता । तपश्चरन्तौ सुमहद् वर्षाणां दश पञ्च च ॥ atriḥ prajāpatiḥ śrīmān anasūyā pativratā | tapaścarantau sumahad varṣāṇāṃ daśa pañca ca || Atri, the illustrious Prajāpati, and Anasūyā, the chaste wife, performed intense penance for fifteen thousand years.
4 तपसा तुष्टा देवेशास्त्रयो लोकेश्वराः प्रभो । ब्रह्मा विष्णुश्च रुद्रश्च दर्शनं दत्तवन्तः ते ॥ tapasā tuṣṭā deveśāstrayo lokeśvarāḥ prabho | brahmā viṣṇuśca rudraśca darśanaṃ dattavantaḥ te || Pleased by their austerity, the three Lords of the worlds — Brahmā, Viṣṇu, and Rudra — appeared before them.
5 वरं वरय भद्रं ते इत्युक्तास्ते महर्षये । पुत्रं त्रिमूर्तिस्वरूपं देहि नः परमेश्वर ॥ varaṃ varaya bhadraṃ te ityuktāste maharṣaye | putraṃ trimūrtisvarūpaṃ dehi naḥ parameśvara || They said: “O sage, ask for a boon that is auspicious.” The couple replied: “Grant us a son who bears the form of the Trimurti, O Supreme Lord.”
6 ततोऽब्रुवन् सुराः सर्वे साक्षाद् दत्तोऽत्रयेऽनघ । दत्तात्रेय इति ख्यातो योगीशः सर्वगोचरः ॥ tato'bruvan surāḥ sarve sākṣād datto'traye'nagha | dattātreya iti khyāto yogīśaḥ sarvagocaraḥ || Then all the gods declared: “Directly given to Atri, the sinless one — Dattātreya, renowned as the Lord of Yoga, all-pervading.”
7 स बाल्ये चैव कौमार्ये यौवने च महामुनिः । ज्ञानवैराग्यसम्पन्नो भक्त्या परमया युतः ॥ sa bālye caiva kaumārye yauvane ca mahāmuniḥ | jñānavairāgyasampanno bhaktyā paramayā yutaḥ || From childhood, youth, and beyond, he was a great sage, endowed with knowledge and detachment, filled with supreme devotion.
8 सर्वभूतहिते रतः सर्वज्ञः सर्वदर्शिवान् । त्रिमूर्तिस्वरूपोऽसौ दत्तात्रेयो महायशाः ॥ sarvabhūtahite rataḥ sarvajñaḥ sarvadarśivān | trimūrtisvarūpo'sau dattātreyo mahāyaśāḥ || Ever engaged in the welfare of all beings, omniscient, all-seeing — that Dattātreya of great fame, bearer of the Trimurti form.
9 अनसूयायां जातश्च चन्द्रमा दुर्वासस्तथा । सोमः शिवांशश्च क्रोधी दुर्वासाः स महामुनिः ॥ anasūyāyāṃ jātaśca candramā durvāsastathā | somaḥ śivāṃśaśca krodhī durvāsāḥ sa mahāmuniḥ || Also born to Anasūyā were Chandra (Soma) and Durvāsas — Soma from Viṣṇu’s aspect, Durvāsas from Śiva’s wrathful part, the great sage.
10 दत्तात्रेयस्तु योगीशो विहरत्यचलो यथा । सर्वलोकहितार्थाय धर्मपालनतत्परः ॥ dattātreyastu yogīśo viharatyacalo yathā | sarvalokahitarthāya dharmapālanatatparaḥ || But Dattātreya, the Lord of Yoga, wanders unshaken, ever dedicated to the protection of dharma for the good of all worlds.
11 एवं दत्तात्रेयस्य जन्म कथितं ते मया द्विज । यः शृणोति सदा भक्त्या स गच्छेत्परमं पदम् ॥ evaṃ dattātreyasya janma kathitaṃ te mayā dvija | yaḥ śṛṇoti sadā bhaktyā sa gacchetparamaṃ padam || Thus I have told you, O twice-born, the birth of Dattātreya. Whoever hears this with devotion attains the supreme abode.
Source: Brahmāṇḍa Purāṇa – Uttara Khaṇḍa – Dattātreya Charitam (Adhyāya 38)
