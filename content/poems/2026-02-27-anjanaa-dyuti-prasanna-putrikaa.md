abstract: This devotional poem celebrates the auspiciousness of the Āṣāḍha month and
  invokes the blessings of a divine female figure associated with Hanumān's grace

  to invoke prosperity, learning, and spiritual radiance for the addressee.
preamble: This work belongs to the Telugu devotional poetry tradition, specifically
  poems composed to mark sacred calendrical occasions and lunar auspiciousness (tithi).
  The text engages with Hindu sacred calendar observations, particularly the Āṣāḍha
  month (June–July), which holds significance in the worship of Viṣṇu and in monsoon-related
  rituals. The invocation of Hanumān (Hanumant) and the Vālakhilya ascetics grounds
  the poem in classical Vedic and Purāṇic traditions, while the reference to a feminine
  divine presence suggests syncretism with regional goddess worship. Such occasional
  verses represent an important genre in South Indian literary culture, combining
  ritual propriety with ornate diction.
